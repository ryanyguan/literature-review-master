# Language and Style Rules

## Default manuscript style

Use British English for manuscript prose unless the target outlet explicitly requires another convention.

## Colon and semicolon minimisation

When drafting related work, introduction, discussion, limitation or response prose, avoid colons and semicolons where a natural alternative exists.

Do not change meaning, logical relation, scope condition or academic rigour. Do not chop coherent paragraphs into thin fragments. A paragraph should remain narratively and logically complete.

## Acceptable exceptions

A colon may remain in a title, table label, formal template, mathematical definition, list header, reference metadata, or a sentence where replacement would be awkward or less precise.

A semicolon may remain when removing it would damage a compact parallel structure. Use this rarely.

## Prose quality checks

```text
Is the claim supported by cited literature?
Does the sentence overstate novelty?
Is the gap a mechanism gap rather than a phrase-count gap?
Does the paragraph preserve its intellectual arc?
Are British spellings consistent?
Are colons and semicolons avoidable here?
```
