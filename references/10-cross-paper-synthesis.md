# Cross-Paper Synthesis

## Purpose

Synthesis is not summarisation. It explains how papers relate, conflict, converge or leave a gap.

## Required synthesis matrices

Use these when relevant.

### Framework matrix

| Paper | Actors | Timing | Information | Solution concept | Main result | User-gap relevance |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|

### Evidence matrix

| Paper | Evidence type | Data or model | Strength | Weakness | Use in manuscript |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|

### Gap matrix

| Stream | What is known | What is missing | Why it matters | No-data viability | Risk |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|

### Claim-citation matrix

| Manuscript claim | Supporting citations | Support strength | Risk | Required wording |
|---|---|---|---|---|

## Synthesis moves

Use these intellectual moves.

```text
Convergence, where multiple streams agree.
Conflict, where streams imply incompatible predictions.
Boundary, where one stream holds under a condition another relaxes.
Method transfer, where a method can solve a problem in another field.
Mechanism insertion, where one literature omits a key force.
Theory repair, where a stylised model explains an empirical regularity.
```

## Anti-summary rule

Do not write one paragraph per paper unless the user asks for annotated bibliography. Related work should usually be organised by streams and mechanisms.
