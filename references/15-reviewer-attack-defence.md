# Mode G. Reviewer Attack and Literature Defence

## Purpose

Simulate a hostile reviewer who knows the literature better than the author.

## Attack types

```text
You missed the closest paper.
Your gap is cosmetic.
Your model is a relabelled version of an existing framework.
Your target journal identity is wrong.
Your simulations do not validate the mechanism.
Your citations are from the wrong field.
Your contribution is methodologically thin.
Your no-data design cannot support your claim.
```

## Defence output

| Reviewer attack | Evidence behind attack | Severity | Defence strategy | Manuscript change |
|---|---|---|---|---|

## Defensive writing strategy

Acknowledge close papers before reviewers weaponise them. Make the difference precise. Do not hide adjacent literature. A clear boundary is stronger than an inflated gap.
