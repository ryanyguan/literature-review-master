# Search Scope Ladder

## Principle

Search should be broad enough to catch killer papers and narrow enough to avoid useless noise. Do not search all journals, databases or disciplines with equal weight. Search by rings and adjust the rings to the project identity.

## Ring 1. Direct target-journal and home-discipline core

Start with journals, conferences, books and working-paper channels that define the paper's primary disciplinary identity. The home discipline is the literature that would produce the most dangerous reviewer if ignored.

For operations, management science and optimisation work, prioritise Operations Research, Management Science, Manufacturing and Service Operations Management, Production and Operations Management, INFORMS Journal on Computing, Mathematical Programming, EJOR and other field-defining outlets when relevant.

For marketing, consumer research, advertising, branding and retailing, prioritise Marketing Science, Journal of Marketing, Journal of Marketing Research, Journal of Consumer Research, Journal of Consumer Psychology, Journal of Retailing, International Journal of Research in Marketing and relevant ABS-ranked outlets.

For economics and finance, prioritise the field's leading theory, empirical and methodological outlets. Use finance, economics, accounting, market microstructure, asset pricing, corporate finance, banking or household-finance streams according to the central mechanism.

For information systems, digital platforms, AI applications, human-technology interaction and algorithmic governance, prioritise Information Systems Research, MIS Quarterly, Journal of Management Information Systems, Management Science, relevant computer-science venues and field-specific application outlets.

For statistics, econometrics, machine learning, probabilistic modelling and Bayesian work, prioritise statistical, econometric, computer-science and applied-methodology venues. Separate methodological novelty from substantive application contribution.

For engineering, project management, health, education, public policy, environmental science, energy, sustainability and interdisciplinary research, begin with the outlets that define the problem community before expanding to method-oriented venues.

## Ring 2. Embedded ABS AJG 2024 database

Use `database/abs_2024_3_4_4star_database.csv` to search all bundled AJG 2024 4*, 4 and 3 journals across business and management fields.

Use rating as a priority signal.

```text
4* means highest priority if field fit is real.
4 means strong priority.
3 means useful for coverage, domain detail, and citation completeness.
```

Do not treat an ABS rating as evidence that a paper belongs in the manuscript. Relevance still dominates rank.

## Ring 3. Methodological and technical core

For game theory and mechanism design, search economics theory, industrial organisation, marketing science, operations, political economy, finance, information systems, public policy and applied probability as needed. Include solution-concept keywords such as Nash equilibrium, Bayesian Nash equilibrium, subgame-perfect equilibrium, perfect Bayesian equilibrium, Stackelberg game, Markov-perfect equilibrium, mechanism design and equilibrium selection.

For optimisation algorithms, search mathematical programming, operations research, computer science, approximation algorithms, stochastic programming, robust optimisation, dynamic programming, heuristics and computational benchmarks.

For statistics, econometrics, Bayesian approaches and probabilistic modelling, search statistics, econometrics, machine learning and applied probability outlets. Use method terms and application terms separately.

For machine learning, deep learning, LLMs and AI agents, search top AI conferences, arXiv, ACM, IEEE, statistics, information systems, management, marketing, operations and domain-specific outlets. Separate technical novelty from social, organisational, policy or decision contribution.

For qualitative, mixed-methods or conceptual work, search theory-building, methods and domain outlets. Treat construct definitions, research traditions and interpretive claims as citation-bearing.

## Ring 4. Interdisciplinary prestige layer

Nature, Science, PNAS and their family journals should be searched when the topic has broad scientific, social, AI, behavioural, sustainability, medical, climate, network, complex-systems or human-technology relevance.

Do not force these outlets into a narrowly disciplinary paper unless they directly shape the mechanism, empirical phenomenon, scientific context or policy relevance.

## Ring 5. Working paper and preprint layer

Search SSRN, NBER, arXiv, RePEc, Semantic Scholar, OpenAlex, author websites, institutional repositories and conference proceedings when unpublished or recently accepted work can kill the idea.

Finance, economics, AI, ML, econometrics, statistics and rapidly moving interdisciplinary topics require stronger working-paper scanning than mature literatures with slow publication cycles.

## Keyword architecture

Use four kinds of query.

```text
Direct concept query
Alternative terminology query
Mechanism query
Collision query
```

For example, a platform-service model might require these variants.

```text
platform service quality strategic consumers equilibrium
marketplace pricing service level game theory
operational capacity consumer waiting platform pricing
strategic delay service investment two-sided market
```

For a statistical or computational project, use method and application variants.

```text
Bayesian hierarchical model demand forecasting retail
probabilistic modelling policy evaluation uncertainty
causal machine learning pricing field experiment
algorithmic decision support human AI collaboration
```

Always record search limitations.
